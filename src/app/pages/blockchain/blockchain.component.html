What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a Distributed Ledger Technology spread across a network of computers. It can hold data or smart contracts (program code that executes when certain conditions are met).

Some of its key features are below:

Decentralized

No single entity controls it – multiple nodes exist spread across the world – so no single entity ex: a corporation can manipulate it.

Smart Contracts

A smart contract is an agreement between two parties in the form of program code. They are simply programs stored in the blockchain that run when predetermined conditions are met.

Public/Private Blockchain

The public blockchain is open to the public and anyone can join. A Private Blockchain is more of a customized network created for the specific requirements of the business entity.

Anonymous

The identity of the individual parties is protected. Public Blockchain is verifiable yet anonymous.

Immutable

Once an entry is made – it cannot be removed or changed. The entire history of the blockchain can be traced – forever.

Secure Tamperproof

All records are individually encrypted and cannot be hacked.

Why should you use Blockchain

Immutability or Tamperproof

Once in the blockchain – no one can change it or delete it. They can only add to the chain. This ensures that we can trust that the information was not tampered with and more importantly cannot be tampered with.

Openness or Transparent

There are different types of blockchains but Public Blockchains by their very nature are not hidden. This transparency is useful when we need things to be open. For example, think Elections – blockchain can be implemented in elections (not just national elections but also within smaller groups and organizations). Transparency increases TRUST. Nothing to hide.

Freedom (Digital)

Blockchain is decentralized – no single entity controls it. You control your own money for example. Bitcoin (based on Blockchain) is one such example. No government or organization controls your Bitcoin money.

Decentralized systems also allow for two or more parties to engage with each other with Trust. We trust the process, tech and system (Blockchain) even when we do not know the other party.

Security

All transactions are encrypted. Using Cryptography and Decentralized nodes – data in the blockchain cannot be forged or changed by hackers.

Every block is linked to the previous block and changes cannot be done, blocks can be added but previous blocks cannot be modified.

Costs

Blockchain is less expensive to run compared to other technologies. It is more secure and reduces costs due to improved efficacy.

Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are program codes that executes when certain predefined conditions are met.

It reduces the cost of a transaction by eliminating some of the middlemen and enhancing security

What are Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are simply programs stored on a blockchain that run when predetermined conditions are met. They typically are used to automate the execution of an agreement so that all participants can be immediately certain of the outcome, without any intermediary’s involvement or time loss. They can also automate a workflow, triggering the next action when conditions are met.

Speed, efficiency and accuracy

Once a condition is met, the contract is executed immediately. Because smart contracts are digital and automated, there’s no paperwork to process and no time spent reconciling errors that often result from manually filling in documents..

Trust and transparency

Because there’s no third party involved, and because encrypted records of transactions are shared across participants, there’s no need to question whether information has been altered for personal benefit.

Security

Blockchain transaction records are encrypted, which makes them very hard to hack. Moreover, because each record is connected to the previous and subsequent records on a distributed ledger, hackers would have to alter the entire chain to change a single record.

Costs Saving

Smart contracts remove the need for intermediaries to handle transactions and, by extension, their associated time delays and fees.

Public Blockchain

Public Blockchains tend to be the most secure implementation of Blockchain. Anyone can join Bitcoin for example runs on a implementation of a public blockchain.

Federated Blockchain

Also known as Consortium Blockchain – this type of blockchain implementation occurs when a group of companies come together – form a consortium and run a blockchain network.

Control is shared, distributed among the members of the Consortium.

Private Blockchain

Private Blockchain are implementations of blockchain which is managed and controlled by a single entity.
